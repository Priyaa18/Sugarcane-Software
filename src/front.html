U.P. Co-operative sugar factories Federation as an apex body of U.P. was established in 1963 for development and expansion of co-operative sugar industries in U.P. Sugar federation since its inception has been playing active role in establishment of new sugar factories, expansion and modernisation of existing sugar factories, establishment of distilleries for use of byproducts and giving financial, technical and administrative advices and guidelines. Federation has opened and liberalised its Sugar agent policy. It is now open window system and one can apply any time since tender system has been abolished. Any person having Sugar Sale linence can apply to Managing Director , U.P.Cooperative Sugar Factories Fed Ltd., 9-A Rana Pratap Marg Lucknow on their letter head along with the following information and documents and bank draft Earnest money of Rs. 5 Lacs for 5 mills, Rs. 10 Lacs for 15 mills and Rs. 15 Lacs for all co-operative sugar mills of U.P. We shall not pay any interest on said Earnest Money. Along with this you have to give the names of the mills for which Agency is required in favour of U.P.Cooperative Sugar Factories Federation Ltd. Payable at Lucknow.

Development

The First co-operative sugar factory was installed in year 1957 in district Nainital ( currently in Distt. Udham Singh Nagar). It was inaugurated by India's first Prime Minister Pt. Jawahar Lal Nehru on 16th Feb,1959. Its successful operation brought a new enthusiasm in U.P. and consequently other new co-operative sugar mills were installed. During 1959 to 1971, there were only five co-operative sugar factories in U.P. Currently there are 28 units in U.P. in which 6 in western zone, 14 in central zone and rest 8 in eastern zone. Five Sugar mill namely Dhuriapar, Sathion, Aurai, Powayan & Majhola are not in operation. Thus a total 23 sugar factories are functional in U.P in co-operative sector with total crushing capacity of 60000 TCD.

Products & Byproducts

Sugar

Sugarcane factories are primarily known for producing various types of sugar, including granulated sugar, powdered sugar, and brown sugar. The sugar is extracted from the sugarcane juice through processes such as crushing, boiling, and evaporation.

Molasses

Molasses is a thick, dark syrup that is a by-product of sugar production. It is rich in nutrients and is often used in the production of alcoholic beverages, such as rum and certain types of beer. It can also be used in baking, as a sweetener, or as an animal feed additive.

Bagasses

Bagasse is the fibrous residue that remains after the sugarcane stalks are crushed to extract the juice. It is a valuable co-product of sugarcane factories and is commonly used as a source of renewable energy. Bagasse can be burned to produce steam and generate electricity for the factory's operations or for sale to the power grid.

Ethenol

Some sugarcane factories have distillation facilities to produce ethanol, a biofuel derived from sugarcane juice or molasses. Ethanol is used as a fuel additive or as a standalone fuel, particularly in the production of biofuels for vehicles.

Filter cake

Filter cake is a residual solid material obtained during the sugarcane juice clarification process. It is often dried and used as a nutrient-rich organic fertilizer or animal feed supplement.

Cogeneration and Bioenergy

Cogeneration and Bioenergy: In addition to bagasse, sugarcane factories may generate additional bioenergy by utilizing other agricultural residues or waste products, such as rice husks or wood chips. This energy can be used to power the factory and potentially supply electricity to the surrounding community.

Future Objective

Sugar federation has plan for efficient functioning of sugar mills and distilleries, financial stability and quality control of its products. Emphasis is on maintaining financial discipline, financial control and effective management. Modernisation of sugar mills is being done to improve the quality of products. Besides there is an effective plan for improvement of financial health of sick sugar mills. All this endeavour has however, kept the interest of the cane growers as one of the prime concerns.
